What intakes are available?

Main Intakes: Australian universities commonly have two main intakes. The first intake is in February/March, and the second intake is in July. These are applicable for both undergraduate and postgraduate programs.

Mid-Year Intake: Some universities may also have a mid-year intake in November for certain programs.

What is the duration of the process?

Undergraduate Programs: The application process typically starts in the fall, and the application deadline is usually in January for most courses starting in September. You can expect to receive decisions from universities by the spring or early summer.

Postgraduate Programs: Application deadlines vary but are generally in the fall or early winter for programs starting the following September. Decisions are often communicated within a few weeks to a couple of months after the application deadline.

Do we need an English Language Proficiency score ready to drop the application?

Universities in Australia typically require proof of English language proficiency. The IELTS and TOEFL are widely accepted, but some universities may also accept other tests like the Pearson Test of English (PTE).

Is GRE/GMAT required for admission in Australia?

For course conversion in countries like Australia, Canada, and the UK, the need for GRE or GMAT scores will depend on the specific policies of the university and program. In many cases, changing your major within a program may not require additional standardized test scores, especially if you are already enrolled in a related field.
